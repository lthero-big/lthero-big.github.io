流程图

The diagram below shows the manufacturing process for making sugar from sugar cane. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

The picture illustrates the process of producing sugar from sugar cane. Generally speaking, there are seven main stages in the process, starting with growing sugar canes on farms and ending with drying and cooling sugar.

The first four steps involve the collection and purification of the sugar canes. The process commences with cane being grown over a period of a year before being harvested through mechanical machines and manual methods, which then leads to a crushing process. In this stage, sugar canes are crushed in a grinder, resulting in the formation of juice. Following this, the sugar cane juice is purified through a limestone filter in order to obtain clean syrup. The remaining steps incorporate the evaporation of juice under heat to produce syrup. Subsequently, sugar crystals are separated from the syrup under a centrifuge machine. In the final step, the resulting sugar crystals are dried and cooled to create final sugar.

In contemporary era, an increasing number of firms and manufacturers tend to highlight new features when marketing their new products and services. The essence of this phenomenon is that the younger generation of consumers is often attracted to novel and innovative features of products. I firmly believe that this advertising development can bring many positive aspects to community.

One reason manufacturers emphasize new features of these products is to attract more customers, especially during economic downturns in many different companies. Today’s consumers are looking for products that offer good value, so products and items with more new updated features would attract more interest from people. For instance, many car manufacturers put more emphasis on inner designs and advanced software services like autonomous driving technology; customers are more likely to choose cars with additional useful functions rather than those designed solely for commuting.

Furthermore, the rapid development of technology provides manufacturers and companies with an improved environment to innovate and create products rich in new features. These features would significantly appeal to individuals who may not be the primary target customers. For example, there are smartphones designed specially for children that restrict distracting applications. However, phones incorporating AI functions like ChatGPT are more likely to be popular among children, as these new features not only capture their interest but also assist them with their assignments.

In conclusion, firms’ emphasis on product features can be seen as positive only when the features are genuinely new and useful. However, if they are not, companies will set a high bar and might lose consumer trust.
